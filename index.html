User Perception of Privacy Apps

This survey was conducted as part of a larger study exploring user perception of privacy apps and the usability of privacy apps. Conducted by Carleton's Human Oriented Research in Usable Security Group.

  • Date: December 2015
  • Topic: Mobile Usability and Privacy
  • Publication: Hala Assal, Stephanie Hurtado, Ahsan Imran, and Sonia Chiasson. What’s the deal with privacy apps? A comprehensive exploration of user perception and usability. In Mobile and Ubiquitous Multimedia (MUM). ACM, 2015.

Driver Distraction & Unfamiliar Road Signs

This report is a summary of research conducted as part of my Master's thesis in which I explore drivers' behaviour when they encounter road signs from different countries. My motivation is to improve driver safety and minimize distraction through the improvement of in-vehicle systems.

  • Date: September 2015
  • Topic: Safety in Automotive User Interfaces
  • Publication: Stephanie Hurtado. An eye-tracking evaluation of driver distraction and road signs. Master’s thesis, School of Computer Science, Carleton University, 2015.
